Weekly routine

A simple system: real input every day, forced output every day, feedback every week.

60 min / day

Input layer

  • Reading: articles → academic texts → paper intros.
  • Listening: lectures, podcasts, TOEFL lectures.
  • Keep a small list of useful phrases, not isolated words only.
45–60 min / day

Output layer

  • Write 150–300 words or one timed TOEFL-style answer.
  • Record 5–10 minutes of speaking and review mistakes.
  • Re-use topics from CS, university, applications, and research.
30–45 min / day

Correction layer

  • Anki/spaced repetition for high-value vocabulary.
  • Grammar fixes from your own writing and speaking errors.
  • Weekly mock or timed section from Month 4 onward.

6-month timeline

Each month has a single job. Do not optimize everything at once.

01
Foundation resetB1 → strong B1

Make the habit automatic and remove basic friction

  • Review high-impact grammar: tenses, articles, prepositions, relative clauses, conditionals.
  • Build a 1,000-word active deck for academic/core vocabulary.
  • Read 20 short articles and write 12 summaries of 120–180 words.
  • Record 20 short speaking answers on everyday, university, and CS topics.
DeliverableDiagnostic test + 12 summaries + 20 recordings + personal error list.
02
Fluency buildingB1+ → B2

Stop translating; start thinking in English

  • Read longer texts about technology, AI, education, productivity, and research.
  • Listen to 4–5 hours/week of clear English lectures or podcasts.
  • Write 3 structured paragraphs/week: claim, evidence, conclusion.
  • Do 3 speaking sessions/week: 2 minutes prepared + 2 minutes spontaneous.
Deliverable8 paragraph essays + 12 speaking answers + 20h listening log.
03
Academic transitionB2

Move into university, CS, and research English

  • Read one CS/AI blog post or paper abstract every day.
  • Summarize 2 research papers/week in simple, clear English.
  • Write one 350–450 word essay/week and revise it once.
  • Practice lecture note-taking: main idea, details, examples.
Deliverable8 research summaries + 4 revised essays + 5-minute CS talk.
04
Exam modeB2 → B2+

Train TOEFL-style timing while keeping real fluency

  • Weekly TOEFL-style reading set and listening set.
  • Two timed speaking prompts/week with clear structure.
  • Two writing tasks/week: one independent, one integrated.
  • Use connectors naturally: however, therefore, whereas, in contrast.
DeliverableTOEFL practice log + recurring mistake bank + mini mock.
05
Speed & polishB2+ → C1-

Improve precision, speed, and confidence

  • Do one full timed section every week.
  • Write application texts: CV bullets, professor email, SOP paragraphs.
  • Give one 7–10 minute presentation/week about AI, IR, or your project.
  • Extract academic phrases from at least 4 paper introductions.
DeliverablePresentation recordings + polished email/SOP drafts + phrase bank.
06
C1 readinessC1 target

Convert training into test and application performance

  • Take 2 full mock tests: start and end of the month.
  • Finalize self-introduction, research summary, professor email, and SOP draft.
  • Practice 45–60 second answers and 3–5 minute research explanations.
  • Book TOEFL/IELTS when mock scores are stable enough.
Deliverable2 mock tests + final application English pack + exam decision.

Example week

A compact week template. Repeat it, then increase difficulty each month.

Mon

Reading + vocabulary + short speaking. English class day: review class notes.

Tue

Listening notes + 250-word writing. Correct grammar from your own text.

Wed

CS article/paper abstract + summary. Record a 3-minute explanation.

Thu

TOEFL-style prompt + English class. Save new mistakes to error bank.

Fri

Longer essay or integrated writing. Revise once after feedback.

Sat

Mock section or timed practice. Check speed, not just correctness.

Sun

Weekly review: update tracker, choose next week topics, clean vocabulary.

Rules

Non-negotiable

Every day

  • Read something real.
  • Listen to something real.
  • Produce English: write or speak.
  • Review 20–40 useful cards.
Review loop

Every week

  • One longer writing task.
  • One recorded speaking review.
  • One academic/CS topic summary.
  • One honest progress check.
