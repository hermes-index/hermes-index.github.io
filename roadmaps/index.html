Roadmap 001 · English C1

B1 → C1 English
in 6 months

A practical roadmap to move from an intermediate B1 level toward C1-level academic and professional English, with TOEFL-style skills, daily consistency, feedback loops, and measurable weekly outputs.

6 months24 focused weeks
2–3h/dayminimum effective workload
C1 targetacademic + application-ready

North star

By the end, you should be able to read papers, write clean academic emails/SOP drafts, follow lectures, speak clearly about CS/research topics, and take TOEFL/IELTS preparation seriously.

Start level: B1, roughly comfortable with everyday English but not yet academic fluency.
Target: C1-like performance, especially for university applications and research communication.
Method: input every day, active recall, speaking/writing feedback, and monthly mock tests.

Weekly structure

Daily · 60 min

Core input

  • 30 min reading: graded articles → academic articles → papers.
  • 30 min listening: podcasts, lectures, TOEFL lectures.
Daily · 45–60 min

Active output

  • Write 150–300 words or one TOEFL-style answer.
  • Speak 5–10 minutes, record, then correct mistakes.
Daily · 30–45 min

Language engine

  • Vocabulary with Anki/spaced repetition.
  • Grammar fixes from your own mistakes, not random exercises.

6-month roadmap

Each month has one main goal. Do not skip output: writing and speaking are what turn passive B1 knowledge into C1 control.

Month 1B1 → strong B1Foundation reset

Stabilize grammar, pronunciation, and habit

  • Review high-impact grammar: tenses, articles, prepositions, relative clauses, conditionals.
  • Build a 1,000-word active academic/core vocabulary deck.
  • Read 20 short articles and write 12 summaries of 120–180 words.
  • Record 20 short speaking answers: self-introduction, university, CS, goals, daily topics.
  • End-of-month test: one complete reading/listening diagnostic + one writing sample.
Month 2B1+ → B2Fluency building

Move from translating to thinking in English

  • Read longer texts: technology, education, psychology, AI, research news.
  • Listen to 4–5 hours/week of clear English lectures or podcasts.
  • Write 3 paragraphs/week with a clear claim, evidence, and conclusion.
  • Do 3 speaking sessions/week: 2 minutes prepared + 2 minutes spontaneous.
  • Start TOEFL-style integrated tasks lightly: listen/read → summarize.
Month 3B2Academic transition

Shift toward university and research English

  • Read one CS/AI blog post or paper abstract every day.
  • Summarize 2 research papers/week in simple English.
  • Write one 350–450 word essay/week; revise it once after feedback.
  • Practice note-taking from lectures: main idea, details, examples.
  • End-of-month: mini mock test + 5-minute talk about a CS topic.
Month 4B2 → B2+TOEFL/academic mode

Train exam skills without losing real fluency

  • Weekly TOEFL-style reading set and listening set.
  • Two speaking prompts/week with timing; focus on structure and clarity.
  • Two writing tasks/week: one independent, one integrated.
  • Start using academic connectors naturally: however, therefore, whereas, in contrast.
  • Collect recurring mistakes into a personal correction list.
Month 5B2+ → C1-Polish and speed

Improve speed, precision, and confidence

  • Do one full timed section every week: reading, listening, speaking, or writing.
  • Write application-related texts: CV bullets, research email, SOP paragraphs.
  • Give one 7–10 minute presentation/week about AI, IR, or your research project.
  • Read at least 4 paper introductions and extract useful academic phrases.
  • Reduce filler words and Persian sentence patterns in speech.
Month 6C1 targetMock + application readiness

Convert training into test/application performance

  • Take 2 full mock tests, one at the start and one near the end of the month.
  • Finalize reusable academic assets: self-introduction, research summary, professor email, SOP draft.
  • Practice speaking under pressure: 45–60 sec answers and 3–5 min research explanations.
  • Write 4 polished essays or application texts and revise them deeply.
  • Decide final exam target and book TOEFL/IELTS when mock scores are stable.

Non-negotiable habits

Every day

  • Read something real.
  • Listen to something real.
  • Produce English: write or speak.
  • Review 20–40 vocabulary cards.

Every week

  • One longer writing task.
  • One recorded speaking review.
  • One academic/CS topic summary.
  • One honest progress check.

C1 in 6 months from B1 is ambitious. It is realistic only with consistent output, feedback, and at least 2 hours of focused work most days. If the workload drops, keep the same roadmap but extend it to 9–12 months.
